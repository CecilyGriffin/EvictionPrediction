Pair Plot




For the machine learning portion of our project, we decided to first plot a pair plot using seaborn with our data to find potentially interesting relationships to examine further. As you can see, there are lot of relationships to explore in our data, but we were most concerned with the relationship between race, education, familial status, and evictions.


After assembling and cleaning the dataset, we created single linear regressions for each of the elements we were interested in examining further. We also ran a multivariate linear regression in order to see if there was any predictive value in combining the two demographic factors with the highest r-squared scores from our linear regression. From there we created a residual plot.


Our models resulted in the following scores:
Evictions vs % Minority Population 0.26933930267062645
Evictions vs % of Single Female Households with Children: 0.24105539414019683
Evictions vs % of Households with Less Than HS Diploma: 0.1464574308797829
Evictions vs % Minority Population and % Single Female Households with Children:
Training Score: 0.2325314927657959
Testing Score: 0.26769520019577986
